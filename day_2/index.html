Day 2 : 2D Modeling

Based on my previous experience, I chose OnShape as the tool I used. During class period, I completed a simple exercise following a tutorial video. This is the final result.  

These are the three parts I chose from the kit for this exercise. 


While the other two are relatively straight forward, I stuggled at first with recreating the wheel. I tried multiple ways to mimic the smallers parts around its main body. I observed that they are presented is different positions for both sides of the wheel, so I started off with a groups of concentric circles according to the measurements I took with the caliper.


I then used "extrude" to create the hollow part of the wheel. As I notice that the center parts should not be extruded all the way through, I did "remove" to dig the holes half way.


To create the smaller parts around, I learned new tools including "project","create a new plane via offset" and "circular pattern". I split my circle into 32 equal parts, and as I project half of them onto the plane I created, I had 16 on each side of the wheel, which I then arranged to every other space. I had another problem of having multiple parts in one part studio. I fixed it by using "Boolean" and substiting "extrude-add" for "extruded-new".
